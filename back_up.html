SimFlow: Simplified and End-to-End Training of Latent Normalizing Flows

Paper

Overview

We propose SimFlow to improve the performance of Normalizing Flows (NFs). Our key design is to fix the variance (which would otherwise be predicted by the VAE encoder) to a constant (e.g., 0.5).

  • Simple: this method allows the encoder to output a broader distribution of tokens and the decoder to learn to reconstruct clean images from the augmented token distribution, avoiding additional noise or denoising design in STARFlow
  • End-to-end: fixed variance simplifies the VAE evidence lower bound, making it stable to train an NF with a VAE jointly.
  • Good performance: on the ImageNet 256x256 generation task, our model SimFlow obtains a gFID score of 2.15, outperforming the prior work STARFlow (gFID 2.40). Moreover, SimFlow can be seamlessly integrated with REPA-E method and achieves an improved gFID of 1.91, setting a new state of the art among NFs.

Key Insights

VAEs with fixed variances are more robust to latent perturbation.

'Learnable' indicates a standard VAE with learnable variance, while \(\bar{\sigma}^2 = x^2\) denotes a VAE with a fixed variance of \(x^2\).

  • (a) A VAE with a large and fixed variance can maintain reconstruction quality under latent noise, while the performance of a VAE with learnable variance degrades significantly.
  • (b) For VAEs with a large variance, the images reconstructed from linearly interpolated latents still clearly show the main subjects (the cat or the dog), rather than blending them.

End-to-end training makes latent space more suitable for developing generative models.

  • (a) Spectral entropy measures the randomness of frequency components; lower values indicate simpler data distributions in the frequency domain.
  • (b) End-to-end latent sapce has a lower ratio of high-frequency components.
  • (c) Total variation captures the overall local changes across tokens; lower values imply smoother latents.
  • (d) Autocorrelation reflects how similar a token sequence is to a shifted version of itself; higher autocorrelation indicates stronger spatial consistency.

Variant studies.

  • Only setting a fixed variance is not enough.
  • Good reconstruction does not mean good generation quality.
  • The end-to-end training significantly improves the generation quality.
